The advent of individual and absolute ownership in land was an important aspect of modern statecraft. An extant scholarship postulates that local traditional elites, such as landlords or chiefs, have opposed the introduction freehold tenure or cadastral mapping out of fear of losing customary privileges and autonomy from the state. Political rulers in Western Europe had to initiate long-term campaigns to defeat local powerholders and impose these schemes. But in much of the postcolonial world, where bureaucratic capabilities are low, the support of nonruling classes to changes in the rules of land tenure became critical.
